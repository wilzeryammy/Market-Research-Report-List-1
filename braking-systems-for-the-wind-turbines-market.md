<p><p>Braking systems in the wind turbine market are essential for controlling motion and ensuring safety during operations. Yaw brakes are used to rotate the turbine's nacelle to face the wind, enhancing efficiency and stability. Meanwhile, rotor brakes are applied to the turbine blades to halt rotation during maintenance or emergency situations, ensuring safe handling and inspection. Both braking types play crucial roles in optimizing performance and safeguarding the structural integrity of wind turbines in various environmental conditions.</p></p>
