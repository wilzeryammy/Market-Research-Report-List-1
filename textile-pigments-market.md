<p><p>The textile pigments market encompasses various types, each serving unique applications in fabric coloration. Disperse pigments are primarily used for polyester and synthetic fibers, offering vibrant hues. Reactive pigments bond chemically with fibers, ideal for cotton and other cellulosics. Sulfur pigments provide excellent lightfastness for dark shades, typically used in denim. VAT pigments are known for their versatility and stability, suitable for various textiles. Acid pigments cater to protein fibers like wool and silk, while others cover specialized uses across diverse materials.</p></p>
