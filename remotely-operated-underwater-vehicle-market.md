<p><p>The Remotely Operated Underwater Vehicle (ROV) market is primarily divided into two types: intervention and observation. Intervention ROVs are designed for tasks that require manipulation or interaction with the underwater environment, such as repair, maintenance, and installation in various industries like oil and gas. In contrast, observation ROVs are equipped with cameras and sensors for surveying and inspection purposes, focusing on data collection and monitoring marine life or infrastructure without physical interaction. Each type serves distinct operational needs in underwater exploration.</p></p>
